Midcap RS-120 Regime Momentum · SMOOTHEST variant with keep-top-8 risk-off

The whole system runs on two clocks that do different jobs. Get those two clocks straight and every rule below falls into place.

Risk-ON / kept holding Risk-OFF (market weak) Sold → cash @6.5% Fully invested (15)

1  The two clocks

Clock A · runs every WEEK
Regime check

Each week-end: is NIFTYBEES ≥ its 100-day average?

→ Decides are we allowed to hold equity this week. This is the fast clock — it can pull you out quickly.
Clock B · runs every MONTH
Stock selection

Each month-end: rank by strength, apply the top-22 buffer, choose the 15 names.

→ Decides which stocks to buy. This is the slow clock — it's the only time new money goes in.

The regime clock can flash on and off many times between two month-ends. New buying happens only on the slow monthly clock.

5  Your exact question: out in week 1, back above in week 2 — do we buy back?

Short answer: no. "Allowed to invest" is not the same as "buy now." Watch the holdings count — it does not jump back to 15 just because the market recovered:

A market that pops back above the line in week 2 does not drag you back in. Only the month-end snapshot moves money. That gap is the whipsaw brake.

6  The one idea to remember

Permission ≠ Purchase.

The weekly clock can say "you're allowed to hold equity again" — that's just the door unlocking. The actual buying only happens on the slow monthly clock. So a brief one- or two-week bounce above the line costs you nothing — no buy, no churn.

Exit is FAST

Weekly clock. The week the market breaks down, you sell the weakest 7 and raise cash. You don't wait around to bleed.

Re-entry is SLOW

Monthly clock. You only rebuild to 15 at a month-end that is risk-ON. A head-fake recovery can't yank you back in to buy a bounce that fails.

This asymmetry — exit fast, re-enter slow — is why the strategy de-risks in a real selloff yet doesn't churn on noise. It's the whole reason keep-top-8 holds its risk-adjusted return.
