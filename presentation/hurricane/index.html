03 · The routeThe Hurricane
Why you can't stay home

Inflation is the invisible current.
It takes you backwards while you sit still.

Rs. 1 lakh in your savings account today.

In 12 years at 6% inflation, it buys Rs. 50,000 worth of things.

FDs at 7% → ~5% post-tax ≈ inflation. You're treading water. Not arriving anywhere.

Staying home feels safe. It isn't — it's just slow.

04 · InflationThe Hurricane
Three responses to the same storm

People do one of three things when they see the weather.

● Stay home
Cash · FD · real estate only.
Feels safe. Inflation eats it quietly over 20 years. You retire with less than you saved.
● Fly around
Time it. Chase it. Panic out.
Get hit by turbulence anyway. Miss the best ten days of each decade. Arrive late, if at all.
● Fly through
Systematic. Diversified. Long.
Carry instruments. Keep flying on red days. Arrive — with compounded returns and sleep.

05 · Three responsesThe Hurricane
What the hunters carry

Five instruments.
That's the whole kit.

  • Time horizon.   The one advantage retail has over institutions. Compounding works when you own decades.
  • Diversification.   Spread across 50+ stocks via an index. Not 5 tickers you read about on Twitter.
  • SIP.   Monthly buys, automated, same date. Let the market time you.
  • Index core.   70% of your equity in a broad index — Nifty 50, Nifty Next 50, or total-market. The core nobody fires you from.
  • Rebalance.   Once a year. Trim winners, top up losers. Boring. Effective. Two hours of work for one of the biggest wins.
06 · InstrumentsThe Hurricane
What the hunters don't do

Five traps.
Most portfolios die in one of these.

  • Market timing.   Missing the 10 best days in a decade cuts long-run return by roughly half. You won't be right more than wrong.
  • Gurus.   Anyone with a target price and no skin in your outcome is selling you dreams. Including on YouTube.
  • FOMO.   The trend you're hearing about is two months old. By the time it reaches your feed, the early money is getting out.
  • F&O without a system.   Leverage magnifies your worst month. Without written rules, you'll find it.
  • All-in on one stock.   The stock you love will betray you. Every era has a Yes Bank, a DHFL, a Vodafone-Idea.
07 · TrapsThe Hurricane
The most misunderstood word in investing

Volatility is the weather.
Risk is not arriving.

A 30% drawdown in year 2 of a 20-year plan is weather.

Selling because of that 30% drawdown is risk.

The hunters don't fear turbulence. They fear the pilot who turns back.

09 · DrawdownsThe Hurricane
The hunter's instrument

A SIP doesn't have moods.
That's the whole point.

WHEN THE MARKET IS GREEN

Your SIP buys fewer units at a higher price.

WHEN THE MARKET IS RED

Your SIP buys more units at a lower price.

The worst months for discretionary traders are the best months for a SIP.

Red days are not panic. They are buying opportunities you already committed to taking.

13 · AllocationThe Hurricane
Your flight plan · what you do Monday

Seven steps. Four of them take one hour total.
The other three are discipline, not action.

  • Open a demat + MF account · Zerodha · Groww · ICICI Direct · Kuvera
  • Automate a SIP into an index fund · monthly · same date every month
  • Write down your allocation (70/20/10 or whatever fits your age). Keep the paper.
  • Turn off daily P/L notifications · delete the tracker app from the home screen
  • Review quarterly · not daily, not weekly, quarterly
  • Rebalance once a year · put a date on the calendar · two hours of work
  • Never stop the SIP.   Not in March 2020. Not in 2008. Not in the next crash. Never.
14 · Flight planThe Hurricane
The single-stock temptation

The one stock you love
will eventually betray you.

For every Infosys story your uncle tells, there's a Yes Bank, a DHFL, a Vodafone-Idea he doesn't.

Concentration is a lever. It can 10× your return. It can also 10× your regret.

If you must pick stocks, do it with the 10% satellite. The other 90% belongs to the index. That way your best idea can shine, and your worst idea can't sink you.
