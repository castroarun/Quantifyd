Stock drops 20% from its peak since entry (ATH trailing exit). Checked at semi-annual rebalance (Jan & Jul).

Chapter 4

Capital Priority Rules

When multiple signals fire simultaneously, these rules determine allocation.

  • 1
    Full Pool Deployed All Rs.40L is available for trades. No buffer lock — idle cash earns 6% in liquid fund until allocated. Every rupee works.
  • 2
    IPO Scalper Gets Top Priority When capital is tight, IPO Scalper takes available capital first. Rationale: 84.3% WR and +5.5% expectancy vs KC6's 65% WR and +1.7%. One good IPO Scalper trade = 3 KC6 trades.
  • 3
    IPO Swing Gets Second Priority IPO Swing (64.5% WR, +16.17% expectancy) gets second priority after Scalper. These are big IPO runners with listing gain + breakout above ATH. Rs.5L per position, max 2 concurrent.
  • 4
    KC6 Fills Remaining Slots After IPO Scalper and IPO Swing, KC6 trades take whatever capital remains. KC6 recycles faster (7-15 day holds) so freed capital quickly returns to the pool.
  • 5
    Skip When Insufficient If available capital is less than the position size, skip the entry entirely. Never reduce position size to squeeze in a trade — maintain fixed sizing for consistent risk management.

The takeaway: During the 2008 GFC (a 1-in-50-year event), both MQ and Nifty suffered ~60%+ drawdowns — no equity strategy survives that unscathed. But in the COVID era (2015–2025), MQ’s 20% ATH trailing exit limited the worst drawdown to 34% vs Nifty’s 39%. Smart money management plays a role too — when stocks are exited, idle cash isn’t sitting dead. It’s parked in NiftyBEES ETF when Nifty is above its 200-day SMA (riding the trend), or in a liquid debt fund when below (preserving capital). This means even the “cash” portion of the portfolio is working. Adding KC6 tactical trading and IPO strategies further diversifies and cushions the portfolio.

Chapter 7

Daily Workflow

The system runs on three time blocks every trading day.

9:15 – 9:30 AM
Morning Setup
  • Sync KC6 positions with broker (Zerodha)
  • Place KC6 SELL LIMIT targets at today's KC6 mid
  • Check for new IPO listings in past 50 days
  • Check IPO Swing positions for target/SL levels
  • Review overnight corporate actions
3:15 – 3:25 PM
Afternoon Execution
  • KC6: Check target fills + SL/TP/MaxHold exits
  • IPO Scalper: Check +6% target / -20% SL / 20d time exit
  • IPO Swing: Check +30% target / -15% SL exits
  • Scan for new KC6 entries (crash filter check first)
  • Scan for IPO Scalper breakout signals on recent listings
  • Scan for IPO Swing signals (listing gain + BRK7 above ATH)
  • Execute entries via tactical pool allocation
3:30+ PM
Post-Market Reconciliation
  • Verify all order fills and rejections
  • Return freed capital to debt fund bucket
  • Save daily pool snapshot (equity curve data)
  • Log all activity for audit trail
