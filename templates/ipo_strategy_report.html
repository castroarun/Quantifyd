Chapter 1

The IPO Launch Strategy:
Clear Rules, No Guesswork

Buy stocks within their first 50 trading days when price breaks the initial all-time high. Simple entry, systematic exit, no opinions.

1

Wait for the IPO

Stock must be 10-50 trading days old. Too early = no price history. Too late = momentum spent.

2

Detect ATH Breakout

Close must exceed the highest high of the first 10 days. This is the first time price has ever been this high.

3

Confirm Quality

Listing Gain (day 1 close > open) + Breakout ≥3% above initial ATH. This is the only filter that matters.

4

Size the Position

5% of capital per trade. Sequential execution on Rs 1 Crore starting capital.

5

Exit by Rule

Fixed target (+6%), trailing SL, time exit (20 days), or wide stop loss (-20%). Pick your style.

Why IPO Breakouts Work

Newly listed stocks have no overhead resistance. When they break their initial ATH, it signals genuine institutional demand — IPOs that list positive AND break out 3%+ are the strongest momentum stocks in the market. The strategy captures this explosive first-breakout energy before the stock "settles" into normal trading.

Why are RSI/EMA/ADX/MFI useless? Because a stock breaking its ATH for the first time is, by definition, in a strong uptrend with elevated momentum indicators. These filters are redundant with the breakout signal itself.

Finding #2: Win Rate is driven by Target/SL Ratio, NOT entry filters

The breakthrough to 65%+ came from asymmetric R:R — making the SL much wider than the target. A T5/SL20 setup (SL = 4x target) achieves 86.3% WR vs 53.6% for T30/SL15 (SL = 0.5x target).

Finding #3: Time-capped exits produce the highest Profit Factors

Holding for exactly 20 days with a wide SL (no fixed target) captures natural momentum without capping the upside. LG+BRK3+TIME20d/SL20 achieves PF 4.25 at 66.7% WR — the best risk-adjusted system.

Survivorship Bias:
The Reality Check

Our first results on Nifty 500 stocks showed 73% win rate and PF 5.3. When we expanded to all 1,151 NSE IPOs, the truth was sobering.

The Bias Effect
Same strategy, dramatically different results on different universes.
Metric Nifty 500 (145) All NSE (1,151)
Avg trades/config 44 270 (+6x)
Avg win rate 57% 45% (-12pp)
Avg profit factor 3.15 1.98 (-1.17)
Statistical reliability Poor Good

Why the Original Results Were Inflated

  • Nifty 500 stocks are companies that became large and successful — their IPO breakouts naturally worked
  • Failed IPOs (collapsed, delisted) weren't in the database at all
  • Volume filter appeared powerful because it was filtering already-good stocks

What Survived the Expansion

The directional edge is real (PF consistently 1.5–2.8). The strategy genuinely identifies a profitable entry point. And with LG+BRK3% filters + asymmetric R:R, we pushed the expanded universe to 86.3% WR.

Chapter 9

Caveats & Limitations

No backtest is perfect. Here's what could break in live trading.

1. Partial Universe Coverage

While expanded to 1,621 symbols, many NSE micro/nano-cap IPOs may still be missing from yfinance. Stocks that delisted quickly or changed ticker may not have data.

2. No Slippage or Market Impact

IPO stocks in their first 10-50 days may have wide spreads and thin order books. Backtest uses closing prices with no slippage. Especially problematic for micro-cap IPOs.

3. Sequential Capital Assumption

Trades are sized as 5% of current capital, executed sequentially. In practice, multiple IPOs may signal simultaneously. With ~27 trades/year, concurrent positions are likely.

4. Bull Market Period

2015–2025 was a strong bull market for Indian equities, especially mid/small-caps and IPOs. The 2021–2022 IPO boom may skew results.

5. Negative Median Returns

Most configs have negative median returns. The strategy's edge comes from fat-tailed winners. This requires strict systematic execution — cherry-picking "good" setups will underperform.

6. Small Sample on Filtered Systems

LG+BRK3% systems have only 51 trades over 10 years. While the metrics are strong, this is a limited sample for statistical confidence. The unfiltered systems (259+ trades) are more statistically robust.
