The ATH Filter Trap
After a crash, the ATH proximity filter (within 10% of 52-week high) blocks ALL new entries because every stock is 30–50% below its high. The system sits at 1–3 stocks for 15+ months, missing the entire recovery rally.
Weekly Tranches Save 5.4% DD
Instead of buying NIFTYBEES all at once on the first day below 200 SMA, buying 25% each week gives a much better average cost through the crash. MaxDD improves from 29.8% to 24.4% with zero CAGR sacrifice.
Drop-Based Waits Too Long
Waiting for 1–5% further drops before each tranche sounds smart, but in practice the market often bounces before the trigger fires. This leaves too much cash earning only 6.5% in debt instead of participating in the recovery via NIFTYBEES.
